Participant Information and Consent

Study title: Personalization of text-to-image generation using human preference feedback

Principal Investigator: Mojmir Mutny

Thank you for considering participation in our research study. Before you begin, please read the following information carefully.

Purpose of the Study: The purpose of this study is to help us understand aesthetic preferences to improve creative AI tools. Your feedback will help us build more personalized and user-centric systems.

Procedures: You will be asked to view a series of images and select the one that best matches a style you have in mind. The task should take approximately 50 minutes to complete. At the end, you will be asked to briefly describe the style you used as your guide.

Voluntary Participation: Your participation is completely voluntary. You can stop at any time without penalty by simply closing the browser window. Only fully completed sessions will be used in the research analysis.

Confidentiality: All data we collect will be fully anonymized. Your responses will be used for research purposes only. The anonymized data will be stored securely and may be used in future publications. We do not collect any identifiable information.

Risks & Benefits: There are no direct benefits or compensation for participating. The primary risk is the loss of your time. All images have been pre-screened to avoid inappropriate content. However, we cannot rule out that the images may be offensive to you. We cannot anticipate your reaction to these images.

Contact Information: If you have any questions about the study, please contact Dr Mojmir Mutny at: mmutny@broadinstitute.org.

ORSP: Should you have any concerns about the study you can reach out to The Office of Research Subject Protection of the Broad Institute at orsp@broadinstitute.org.

By clicking "I Agree" you consent to participating in the study and the statement below:

  • You confirm that you participate in this study on a voluntary basis and can choose not to participate at any time without giving reasons and without any negative consequences.
  • You have been informed about the aims and the procedures of the study as well as potential risks.
